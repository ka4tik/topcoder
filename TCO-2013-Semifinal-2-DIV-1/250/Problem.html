Problem Statement

    

You are given vector <int>s a, b and an int N. A positive integer x is called good if it satisfies "x is a multiple of a[i] but not a multiple of b[i]" for at least one i. Return the count of all good numbers between 1 and N, inclusive.

Definition

    
Class:GoodNumbers
Method:count
Parameters:vector <int>, vector <int>, int
Returns:int
Method signature:int count(vector <int> a, vector <int> b, int N)
(be sure your method is public)

Limits

    
Time limit (s):2.000
Memory limit (MB):64

Constraints

-N will be between 1 and 1,000,000,000, inclusive.
-a will contain between 1 and 10 elements, inclusive.
-a and b will contain the same number of elements.
-Each element of a and b will be between 1 and N, inclusive.

Examples

0)
    
{4}
{3}
20
Returns: 4
4, 8, 12, 16 and 20 are multiples of 4, but 12 is a multiple of 3. Thus 4, 8, 16 and 20 are good numbers.
1)
    
{3,2}
{2,3}
9
Returns: 5

3 and 9 satisfy the condition for i=0.

2, 4 and 8 satisfy the condition for i=1.

2, 3, 4, 8 and 9 are good numbers.

2)
    
{5,15,5,15}
{4,4,2,2}
50
Returns: 8
3)
    
{1}
{1}
1000
Returns: 0
No number satisfies the condition.
4)
    
{168120222,756,408,194,681,856,964,677,250,845}
{809,652,204,532,420,10,640688057,55,174076738,318}
1000000000
Returns: 16328141
